Frequently Asked Questions

Installing an irrigation system can save time, conserve water, and ensure that your lawn and plants receive consistent and adequate moisture. It can also increase the value of your property and reduce water bills by efficiently distributing water.

The best type of irrigation system depends on various factors including the size and shape of your yard, the types of plants you have, and your local climate. Consulting with a professional can help you choose between options like drip irrigation, sprinkler systems, or a hybrid approach.

It's recommended that irrigation systems be serviced at least once a year. Regular maintenance checks at the start and end of each irrigation season can help ensure the system is working efficiently and not wasting water.

Signs that your irrigation system needs repair include inconsistent water pressure, dry patches on your lawn, unusually high water bills, and visible leaks or puddling around the system. Prompt repairs can prevent further damage and water waste.

While DIY installation is possible, it requires knowledge of plumbing, electrical systems, and local regulations. Hiring a professional ensures that your system is installed correctly and efficiently, potentially saving you time and reducing the risk of costly mistakes.
