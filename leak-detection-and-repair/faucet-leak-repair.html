Understanding Faucet Leak Repair

Faucet leak repair involves several sub-services that address the root of the problem, ensuring long-term solutions:

  • Washer Replacement : A common culprit for leaks, worn-out washers need timely replacement. Our seasoned plumbers excel in selecting the right size and material for durability.

  • Valve Seat Realignment : Improper alignment can cause persistent dripping. Precision realignment by experts prevents further erosion and ensures smooth operation.

  • O-ring Restoration : O-ring failures often lead to leaks around the faucet handle. Swift restoration guarantees leak-tight fittings and extends faucet lifespan.

Frequently Asked Questions

A faucet can leak due to worn-out washers, corroded valve seats, loose parts, or a damaged O-ring. These components can wear out over time or become damaged, leading to leaks.

To fix a leaking compression faucet, first turn off the water supply. Remove the handle, unscrew the packing nut, and replace the old washer with a new one. Reassemble the faucet and turn the water supply back on to check for leaks.

You'll need a few basic tools: an adjustable wrench, a screwdriver, pliers, and replacement parts such as washers or O-rings, depending on the faucet type.

No, it is necessary to turn off the water supply before repairing a leaky faucet to prevent water from flowing while you are working on it. This avoids potential water damage and ensures your safety.

If your faucet is still leaking, you might have used the wrong size of replacement parts, missed replacing a damaged component, or improperly reassembled the faucet. Double-check the parts and assembly, and consider seeking professional help if the issue persists.
