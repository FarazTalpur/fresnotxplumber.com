Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include unusual noises, leaking water, inconsistent water temperature, reduced hot water supply, and discolored or foul-smelling water.

Noises can be caused by sediment buildup, loose components, a failing heating element, or high water pressure.

It's recommended to have your water heater inspected and serviced annually to ensure optimal performance and longevity.

If your water heater is leaking, turn off the power supply and water supply to the heater and contact a professional plumber for repair.

While some minor repairs and maintenance can be DIY, it's generally advised to hire a professional for repairs, especially for electrical or gas water heaters, to ensure safety and proper handling.
